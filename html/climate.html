Current Impact of Climate Change in the USA

Climate change has already caused significant impacts in the USA. The average temperature has increased by 1.8 degrees Fahrenheit since the early 20th century. This has led to more intense heatwaves, wildfires, and other extreme weather events.

Future Impact of Climate Change in the USA

In the future, the USA is expected to experience more frequent and severe weather events, such as hurricanes and droughts. Sea levels are projected to rise by 1-4 feet by 2100, affecting coastal cities. The local ecosystems will face disruptions, impacting biodiversity.

Minimizing the Impact

  • Reduce carbon emissions by transitioning to renewable energy
  • Implement sustainable agricultural and industrial practices
  • Promote energy efficiency and conservation measures

Local Actions Against Climate Change

  • Community tree planting initiatives
  • Local policies promoting green energy
  • Educational programs about sustainability
